# Academic Pages

His fellowship placement at Healthy Neighborhoods Inc. centers on homeowner repair programs, senior support, reinvestment strategy, and the everyday infrastructures of neighborhood stability, a focus that resonates with urban political economy approaches to community development.

He is a technically skilled GIS analyst who designs and executes complex multi stage spatial workflows across ArcGIS Pro and R, often involving geodatabase management, spatial joins, NEAR analysis, summary statistics, and multi level data integration. His academic projects span Baltimore neighborhood dynamics, NYC rat complaint patterns, COVID 19 ZCTA to tract allocations, Overpass Turbo extractions, and large-scale geoprocessing replications, work that aligns with contemporary discussions of spatial data and geographic problem solving.
